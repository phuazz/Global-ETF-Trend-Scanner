Why skip 1-month?

Jegadeesh & Titman (1993): most recent month reverses. Academic standard 12-minus-1 excludes it.

How to Use This for Trade Setups

● Finding Setups (Screening Phase)
1

Filter for GO or LEAN verdicts in the Signal Alignment Matrix (Section 3). These are ETFs where 3+ of 5 independent signals agree — direction, strength, magnitude, and quality all confirm a trend.

2

Prioritise high R² (≥0.7) — these are Dominic’s “clean trends.” A high composite score with low R² means the trend exists but is choppy and harder to ride. Smooth trends are more forgiving on entry timing.

3

Check ADX direction — you want +DI > −DI (bullish) AND ADX ≥ 25 (trending). ADX rising above 25 is the ideal moment: the trend is just gaining conviction.

4

Cross-reference across asset classes. If commodities, EM equities, and FX all show bullish signals, it’s a macro theme. If only one ETF in a class is trending, it’s idiosyncratic — size positions accordingly.

● Entry & Execution (Timing Phase)

Once the scanner identifies a GO/LEAN setup, click the ticker to open the daily chart. Then look for:

Entry checklist (daily chart):
1 Price above 50 EMA (confirms long-term uptrend)
2 Price above 21 EMA (confirms medium-term direction)
3 SuperTrend is green / bullish (confirms daily trend)
4 Look for pullback to 8 EMA as entry zone
5 ADX rising or ≥25 on daily confirms strength

Ideal entry: Price pulls back to touch/near 10 EMA while SuperTrend remains green and price stays above 50 EMA. This is a “buy the dip in an uptrend” setup with trend confirmation.

Stop loss: Below the SuperTrend line or below the most recent swing low. The SuperTrend adapts to volatility (ATR-based), giving you a dynamic trailing stop.

Exit signals:
• SuperTrend flips red (daily) — tighten or exit
• Price breaks below 21 EMA — reduce position
• Monthly SMA signal flips to BELOW — full exit
• ADX drops below 20 — trend is dying, take profits
Key principle: The scanner tells you WHAT to trade (direction + quality). The daily chart tells you WHEN to enter. Never enter on the scanner alone — always confirm with the chart. Entry point discipline matters: deploy after flat/pullback periods, not after strong runs (Clare et al. 2016).
